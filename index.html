Review Types

Three Types of Reviews

Each review type provides a unique perspective on call quality.

👤

Leadership

Managers and directors review calls to set standards and identify coaching opportunities.

👥

Peer

Reps review each other's calls. Anonymized in the dashboard to encourage honest feedback.

🤖

AI-Generated

Automated daily scoring via LLM analysis of call transcripts. Ensures every call gets baseline coverage.

1

Open the Review Queue

The queue shows all available calls filtered by your role. Leaders see all reps; reps see their own calls for peer review. Use column filters to narrow by account, rep, stage, date, or duration.

2

Select a Call to Review

Click any row to open the call workspace with the Gong player on the left and the review form on the right. The Gong player requires Okta SSO — if it shows a login prompt, click the login button and the app will automatically reload the player when you return.

3

Listen and Score

Play the Gong recording while filling out the review form. Score each of the 9 STC categories from 0–5 and optionally add comments. The overall score calculates automatically as the average.

4

Submit Your Review

Hit submit. Your review is saved to the AppDB collection and automatically synced to the dashboard dataset. A success toast confirms the submission and you return to the queue.

5

Check the Dashboard

Switch to the User Dashboard to see aggregated scores, KPI cards for each STC category, score trends over time, and the Top Performers section. Filter by rep, account, date range, or review type.

Scoring Framework

The STC Rubric

Each category is scored 0–5. Click any category to see the scoring criteria.

Scoring Scale: 0 – 5

The overall score is the average of all 9 categories. Scores below 3.0 are flagged for coaching, 3.0–3.9 indicate solid performance, and 4.0+ represents excellence.

1

Current Situation

Did the rep explore where the prospect is today with genuine curiosity?

4–5
High

Genuine curiosity with broad, open-ended questions that let the prospect describe their world.

“Could you walk me through a typical day or week in your role?”
2–3
Mid

Decent but surface-level questions — gets the “what” but not the “how” or “why.”

“What are your top 3 priorities this quarter?”
0–1
Low

Closed-ended or leading questions, or jumps directly to problems without exploration.

“So, are you struggling with lead generation?”
2

Call Preparedness

Did the rep’s questions show specific research into the customer’s company or industry?

4–5
High

Questions show specific research into the customer’s company, industry, or recent news.

“I saw your CEO’s recent article on sustainability — how does that initiative impact your team’s goals?”
2–3
Mid

Basic research (knows company name/product) but lacks deeper insights into their specific situation.

“Since you’re in the software industry, are you seeing X trend?”
0–1
Low

The call felt generic with no evidence of any research or preparation.

“So, tell me what your company does.”
3

Problem Defined

Did the rep co-create the problem definition and guide the prospect to an “aha” moment?

4–5
High

Rep co-creates the problem definition, guiding the prospect to articulate the root cause themselves.

“That’s a good point — I guess the real problem isn’t just the slow reports, it’s that we don’t trust the data.”
2–3
Mid

A problem is identified, but stays at surface level without exploring the root cause.

Customer: “Our reporting is a mess.” Rep: “Okay, so messy reporting is the main issue.”
0–1
Low

Rep accepts a vague symptom or immediately pitches a solution without defining the real problem.

Customer: “Our reporting is slow.” Rep: “Great, our product is super fast!”
4

Impact

Did the rep guide the customer to articulate the quantifiable business impact of the problem?

4–5
High

Rep successfully guides the customer to articulate quantifiable negative consequences in business terms.

“What’s the cost in man-hours per week when your team has to manually fix those data errors?”
2–3
Mid

Discusses qualitative impact (“that must be frustrating”) but fails to quantify in business terms.

“How does that frustration affect team morale?”
0–1
Low

Rep ignores the impact or minimizes the pain, moving on without exploring consequences.

Customer: “It’s a huge headache.” Rep: “Okay. Anyway…”
5

Ideal

Did the rep get the prospect to describe their own ideal future state?

4–5
High

Uses “magic wand” or vision-oriented questions to get the prospect to describe their own desired outcomes.

“If you could wave a magic wand and design the perfect process from scratch, what would it look like?”
2–3
Mid

Leads the witness by framing the ideal state around their own product instead of the prospect’s vision.

“What if you had a dashboard that showed you all these metrics in real-time?”
0–1
Low

Pitches their own product as the ideal solution instead of asking, or skips this step entirely.

“Let me show you what the future could look like with our platform.”
6

Benefit

Did the rep connect discovery findings to outcomes the prospect can internalize?

4–5
High

Reframes features into outcome-oriented questions, forcing the prospect to internalize the value.

“You mentioned your team spends 20 hours a week on this. What would they do with that extra time?”
2–3
Mid

States a logical benefit but fails to explicitly connect it back to a pain the customer mentioned.

“This will help your team be more efficient.”
0–1
Low

Feature-dumps or states generic benefits disconnected from the conversation.

“Our platform has 500+ connectors, AI-powered analytics, and real-time dashboards.”
7

Layering

Did the rep use follow-up questions to peel back layers from operational to strategic?

4–5
High

Uses simple follow-up questions to peel back multiple layers, moving from operational to strategic impact.

“Tell me more about that.” … “What’s the consequence of that when it happens?”
2–3
Mid

Asks one level of follow-up but stops there instead of continuing to dig deeper.

Customer: “The report is slow.” Rep: “Why is that an issue?” Customer: “It delays our meetings.” Rep: “Okay.”
0–1
Low

Accepts the first answer at face value and moves on without probing deeper at all.

Customer: “It’s slow.” Rep: “Got it. Next question…”
8

Demo Preparedness

Was the discovery rich enough to deliver a customized, compelling demo?

4–5
High

Discovery was so rich the rep can deliver a highly customized demo framed as a compelling story.

“Based on what you told me about X, I’m going to show you exactly how we can solve that.”
2–3
Mid

Gathered enough info for a partially customized demo, but it lacks deep context from the conversation.

“I’ll show you our reporting module since you mentioned reports.”
0–1
Low

Discovery was poor, forcing any demo to be a generic “spray and pray” presentation.

“Let me walk you through all of our features…”
9

Quantified Value

Did the rep tie Domo’s value to measurable business outcomes?

4–5
High

Rep ties Domo’s value to specific, measurable outcomes using numbers from the conversation.

“If we cut that 20-hour weekly process to 2 hours, that’s $180K in annual savings for your team alone.”
2–3
Mid

References value in general terms but doesn’t anchor to specific numbers from discovery.

“Customers typically see significant time savings with Domo.”
0–1
Low

No attempt to quantify value, or uses only generic ROI claims disconnected from the prospect’s situation.

“Domo delivers 10x ROI for our customers.”

Platform

Built on Domo Pro-Code

Domo Pro-Code apps are custom HTML/JS/CSS applications that run inside Domo’s environment with direct access to platform APIs.

Client-Side App

The app runs entirely client-side inside Domo’s environment. No server, no SSR, no build step — just static HTML, JS, and CSS files served directly by the platform.

AppDB Collections

A NoSQL document store built into Domo. RevRadio writes reviews here with a 21-field schema defined in manifest.json. Collections auto-sync to datasets for downstream reads.

Datasets & SQL

Domo datasets provide SQL-queryable reads. RevRadio’s review dataset is pre-joined with Gong call metadata, so the dashboard can filter across thousands of records efficiently.

Deploy Pipeline

Development uses domo dev with a local proxy for live testing. Production deploys via domo publish — no CI/CD pipeline needed.
